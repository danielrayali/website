Hulu Ad Mute

The Basic Idea

I pay for Hulu's streaming service. It has a decent amount of content and some I really enjoy watching. Even though I pay for it, they display advertisements. While not ideal, I understand they have to make money somehow. In fact I'm no too interested as it doesn't impact our budget very much.

What is intrusive is the volume of the advertisements relative to the content you're watching. Streaming services aren't regulated like television commercials where there is a maximum volume that can be issued by the commercial. This means (from emperical observation) that many advertisers turn up the volumne on their commercials and make it unbearably loud. This is where this project comes in.

Using TechnologyTM we can mute these ads using some basic optical character recgonition (OCR). Each Hulu advertisement places a very clear circle with a counter in it, and more interestingly the word "Advertisement" next to it in the upper left corner of the display screen. By using a ZedBoard, an HDMI splitter, some software, an infared receiver and an infared transmitter we can make a system that looks for this and mutes the television when detected. It should also unmute the system when its no longer detected.
